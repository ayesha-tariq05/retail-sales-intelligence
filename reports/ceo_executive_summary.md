ShopSmart Retail Performance Audit
Overview: This analysis aimed to identify key drivers of profit and operational bottlenecks within the ShopSmart retail dataset. The audit reveals that while the Technology category leads in revenue, the Furniture category is significantly underperforming in profitability.

Key Findings:

Profitability Leakage: The Furniture category suffers from a "double whammy" of the highest average discount rates (16.8%) and disproportionately high shipping costs ($44.58), resulting in the lowest profit margin (6.8%) among all categories.

Geographic Focus: The Central and North regions are the company's primary profit engines. Conversely, markets like Southeast Asia and Canada are currently yielding minimal returns and require an immediate operational audit.

Customer Prioritization: The Consumer segment is the most valuable, contributing nearly 50% of total profit. Marketing investments should be heavily weighted toward this segment to maximize ROI.

Recommendations: We recommend re-evaluating the furniture pricing strategy to reduce discount dependency and optimizing the logistics chain for high-cost regions to improve overall profit margins.
